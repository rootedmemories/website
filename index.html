What you’ll be able to do

Build your family tree once, then drop in photos, captions, and stories — all connected to the right person so it stays organised as the years go by.

1) Build the tree

Add relatives once. Invite family so they can contribute safely — without the mess of endless group chats.

2) Add memories to people

Photos + stories actually make sense when they’re attached to the right person (with dates and context).

3) Keep it for life

A living archive your kids can open in 10–20 years and still feel like they know everyone.

Privacy-first by design

Your family history isn’t social media. Rooted Memories is private and invite-only. You control who can view, add, and edit — and nothing is public by default.
