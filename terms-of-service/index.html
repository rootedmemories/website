1. About Rooted Memories

Rooted Memories is a private, mobile-only family archive platform operated by ROOTED MEMORIES LTD.

The platform allows families to create private, invite-only family trees where members can upload, organise and preserve photos, names and family stories in a structured archive.

Rooted Memories is not a public social network. Content is not publicly discoverable or indexed.

2. Accounts

To use Rooted Memories, you must create an account using a valid mobile number and any other information requested in the app.

You are responsible for keeping access to your account and device secure.

3. Family Trees

  • Each tree is private and invite-only
  • Each tree must have at least one active administrator
  • Each tree operates independently from other trees

A sole administrator should transfer control before leaving or deleting their account.

4. Administrators

Each tree has one or more administrators.

Administrators may invite or remove members, create and manage leaves, remove content within their tree, and manage the tree itself.

Administrative actions may be logged for security and governance purposes.

5. Identity Records and Claiming

Leaves may be created by a tree administrator or standard user, and may initially be unassigned.

An unassigned leaf may later be claimed by the relevant person when invited to do so.

Once claimed, the identity owner gains control over their profile within the app.

6. Linking Across Trees

At MVP, Rooted Memories may support linking identity records across separate private trees.

Linking connects identity only. It does not automatically merge or share media between trees.

Media remains scoped to the tree in which it was uploaded.

Users may remove media they personally uploaded. Identity owners and tree administrators may also remove content where applicable within their tree.

9. Account Deletion

Users may request permanent account deletion at any time.

Account deletion is processed within seven (7) days.

Deleting an account removes account access and personal profile data from active systems, although some backup data may remain temporarily before automatic overwrite.

10. Tree Deletion

A tree may be deleted by an administrator.

Deleting a tree permanently removes the leaves, media and member associations stored within that tree.

Tree deletion is irreversible once completed.
